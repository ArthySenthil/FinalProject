Books we are reading this Month:

The Very Hungry Caterpillar by Eric Carle
My rating: 5 of 5 stars

This book shows how a tiny caterpillar grows into a beautiful butterfly. Kids enjoy the way Eric Carle describes how the caterpillar eats different food items each day. A good book to introduce numbers, days of the week and different food items.

Moonchap by Mary Murphy
My rating: 5 of 5 stars

Moonchap by Mary Murphy is a book that shows kids how to overcome each other's differences and get along with friends. A dynamic story that uses shapes and encourages children to develop good citizenship.

Read at Home: First Skills: Wilf's Shapes by Roderick Hunt
My rating: 5 of 5 stars

A good book to introduce 2D and 3D shapes. In this story, kids play shapes hunting at Wilf's birthday party. Each one picks a shape and finds the objects of that shape in his house. A great book for beginning readers.

ABOUT

This program is about helping kids read. Here we will be reading age appropriate books and discussing them. This book club will be a fun way to combine the love of a good book with learning English. Kids practice and improve their reading skills along with other language skills like speaking, listening and vocabulary. Parents will find that the more the kid reads, the more confident he/she will feel about speaking to his/her book club mates in English. As a result, their ability to read and speak English will quickly improve! The book club kids will meet twice a week and read/discuss fascinating children's books. This will make them good readers and book lovers.

OUR PROGRAM

What to expect in a typical session?

Each session will be one hour long. The children will be participating in lots of different reading-related activities.

We will be doing the following in class:

1. Classic book read-aloud:

We will read aloud a classic must-read book and get children involved in discussions about the characters, story and illustrations. List of Books - http://readingclub.libib.com

2. Enrichment activities:

After reading, to increase the kid's engagement with the book, we practice shared and choral reading, and art projects.

3. Phonics instruction:

In each class we will teach a formal phonics lesson that teaches phonemic awareness, letter recognition and letter-sound relationship.

4. Reading games:

Children play word games to reinforce phonemic awareness and letter recognition.

PARENTS' CORNER

Why is reading so important?

UNESCO says reading for pleasure is the single most important thing that will make a child successful in life. “Reading for pleasure is the single biggest factor in success later in life, outside of an education. Study after study has shown that those children who read for pleasure are the ones who are most likely to fulfil their ambitions. If your child reads, they will succeed – it’s that simple.” Bali Rai Evidence suggests that children who read for enjoyment every day not only perform better in reading tests than those who don’t, but also develop a broader vocabulary, increased general knowledge and a better understanding of other cultures. As children start to learn to read at school, we can play an important role in helping to keep them interested in books. Here, we would get them interested in books and make it fun.

What are the benefits of reading?

1. Reading aloud helps children acquire early language skills.

2. Reading aloud helps children develop positive associations with books and reading.

3.Reading aloud helps children build a stronger foundation for school success.

4. Books build better brains!
